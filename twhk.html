HONG KONG
Tsui Wah Restaurant

The original Tsui Wah restaurant located in Central District, Hong Kong

When I think of Hong Kong food, Tsui Wah restaurant 翠華餐廳 comes to mind. It is a predecessor of tea cafes from 1967, and it began as a "bing sut"冰室. It is known as a "cha chaan teng", 茶餐廳, which provides local Hong Kong cuisine. It is a staple of Hong Kong local culture, and is a convenient and fast restaurant. Its branch at Central Wellington Road has become a default meeting/gathering place for many people, especially if they need late night suppers after a night of partying in Lan Kwai Fong.

These are a few of Tsui Wah's hallmark dishes.

One of Tsui Wah's signature and Hong Kong's favorite: milk tea (HK$18). It is made with Ceylon tea leaves, and Holland's Black and White evaporated milk.

One of my personal favorites - fish balls and fish cakes in noodle soup (HK$30). These condiments were made daily with over 10 types of fresh fish and yellow eels.

Definitely a mouthwatering favorite and a food that defines Hong Kong - pineapple bun. Tsui Wah toasts the bun and adds a slab of butter in between the bread. It is a pillowy soft bread with a crunchy and sweet top crust.
